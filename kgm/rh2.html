Chapter 151: My Uncle Yule (Part 1)



Under Marin's arrangement, Prince Philip entertained the Teutonic knights in the city in Königsberg for many days. In addition to the Teutonic Knights belonging to the Westphalia-Rhineland Alliance, knights from other regions were also invited.

For example, Teutonic knights from Switzerland, Austria, the Netherlands, Bohemia, Silesia, Pomerania, and Prussia were also invited.

Although, the number and scale of the Teutonic Knights in these areas cannot keep up with the three major forces of Franconia-Swabia League, Westphalia-Rhineland League and Saxony. However, a little adds up. The total number of these scattered Teutonic Knights is still considerable.

For several days, Marin invited the Teutonic knights from the Westphalia-Rhineland region in Königsberg many times and in batches. At the same time, he also entertained knights from other neutral regions. In short, Marin tried his best to invite them as long as they were not the Teutonic Knights of the archrival Franconian-Swabian Alliance and the Teutonic Knights of the direct competitor Saxony.

After inviting all the Teutonic Knights in Königsberg, Marin and Prince Philip rushed to other cities in the Teutonic Knights Kingdom. Especially the Hanseatic cities - Elbing (Elblag), Torn (Torun) and Mermel (Klaipeda) - entertained and wooed the local Teutonic knights.

In fact, in name, the Teutonic Knights are more than just these four Hanseatic cities. However, the other three Hanseatic cities originally directly under the Teutonic Knights - Danzig, Marienburg and Kulm - were annexed by Poland after the Battle of Tannenberg and the "Thirteen Years' War".

Therefore, the Teutonic Knights Kingdom, which originally had 7 directly subordinate Hanseatic cities, only had 4 Hanseatic cities left. The Livonian Knights, which nominally belongs to the Teutonic Knights, actually controls Dorpat (Tartu), Filin (Viljandi), Reval (Tallinn), and Pernau (Parnu). , Riga, a total of 5 Hanseatic cities. However, since the Battle of Tannenberg, the Teutonic Knights began to decline, and the Livonian Knights began to be less obedient. Although it still belongs to the Teutonic Knights in name, it is actually semi-independent.

Even the Grand Master of the Livonian Knights was elected by the local Livonian Knights and had nothing to do with the Teutonic Knights. Moreover, the Livonian Knights do not need to pay taxes to the Teutonic Knights. Only when the Teutonic Knights are attacked by the Polish-Lithuanian Alliance can they send troops to help.

Therefore, the current economic lifeline of the Teutonic Knights country is actually mainly the four remaining Hanseatic cities - Königsberg, Elbing, Thon and Mermel.

The main formal knights of the Teutonic Knights, the knights are mainly distributed in these four Hanseatic cities and nearby. Because these four Hanseatic cities are the main source of tax revenue for the Teutonic Knights and are also the economic lifeline. Therefore, the more than 800 official Teutonic Knights are basically distributed in these four cities and surrounding areas.

Marin continued to launch a food and drink offensive, conquering a large number of Teutonic knights with Chinese food that far exceeded the dark medieval cuisine. They first went to Elbing and Torn in the southwest, and finally, Marin and Prince Philip came to the Hanseatic port city of Memel on the Baltic Sea coast in the north of the Teutonic Knights...

The management of the Teutonic Knights is different from that of other countries. They do not appoint local officials, but adopt a regimental leader system.

Generally, branches of the Teutonic Knights will be established in each region. The one who controls the local area is also the branch leader of the local Teutonic Knights. And these branch leaders are all official Teutonic Knights, sent down by Königsberg.

For example, the sub-head of the Teutonic Knights in Mermel is a Teutonic Knight of Swiss origin, Amsau, who was arranged by the previous grand head. Elbing's branch leader is a Saxon, and Thorn's branch leader is a Rhinelander...

However, this is not important. Because, at the Grand Master Election Conference, no matter if they are branch leaders or ordinary formal Teutonic Knights, each person only has one vote of suffrage.

Therefore, when Marin wins over those formal Teutonic Knights, he doesn't care much about the specific position. He only cares about whether you are an official Teutonic Knight. Of course, Marin will not offend the branch leaders, lest they secretly cause harm.

In Memel, Marin walked through the streets and visited the official Teutonic Knights. Not only did he treat them to a feast, he also secretly gave them gold coins.

Gold coins are usually carried in small bags, and there are only a few dozen gold coins in one generation. However, it is not too little. You know, since the decline of the Teutonic Knights, life has not been easy for the Teutonic Knights. Moreover, within the country of the Teutonic Knights, there was also the Prussian Alliance formed by local Prussian nobles and citizens, which colluded with Poland. Therefore, the economy of the Teutonic Knights country began to decline, and the life of the knights was not easy. Therefore, although a small bag of gold coins cannot make them rich, it can make them live a lot better. Just like in later generations, someone gives you tens of thousands of dollars. Although it is not enough for you to buy a house or a car, it is enough to make you live a longer life...

One day, Marin and Prince Philip were riding horses on the streets of Mermel, preparing to visit a Teutonic knight of Austrian origin who was very influential in the area. When they passed by a pharmacy, they saw a man walking out of it. , a middle-aged man dressed as a knight, holding a bag of medicine in his hand...

At first, Marin just glanced at it casually, but he was quickly attracted to the middle-aged man...

Because, Marin always felt that this face was familiar. So, he couldn't help but ride his horse to follow...

After following for a while, the middle-aged man who looked a little decadent seemed to be irritated. He turned around suddenly, pulled out part of his sword, and said angrily:

"Who are you? Why are you following me?"

"Uh...well...this gentleman, I just think you look familiar..." Marin scratched his head, but he didn't know how to explain it. After all, if you have a big girl behind you, you can still say that your hormones are excessive, but if you have an uncle behind you, how do you explain it to Marin?

The middle-aged man was also stunned for a moment. He looked at Prince Philip carefully and found that he was not impressed. Then, he looked at Marin carefully...

As a result, the middle-aged man was also a little stunned. Because he also felt vaguely familiar with Marin, as if he had seen him somewhere...

"Excuse me, are you a knight from Westphalia?" The middle-aged man thought for a moment and thought that Marin might be a knight from his hometown, or a descendant of an acquaintance.

You know, the country of the Teutonic Knights is the longing place for many wandering knights in the German region. So it's not surprising that there are some wandering knights from the home region. Although Marin was well dressed, he did not wear the long-distance running silk that symbolized his aristocratic status. Instead, he dressed more like a knight. Similarly, in order to get close to the Teutonic Knights, Prince Philip did not wear silk for long-distance running. Instead, like Marin, he wore a knight's attire, which made it impossible for middle-aged people to judge the origin.

"Uncle Mark? It can't be such a coincidence, right?" Prince Philip and Marin looked at each other, what a coincidence...

"What a coincidence, we are both from Count Mark..." Prince Philip's father and brother are both Earl Mark...

"My name is Jules Hofmann, from Bochum, Counties of Mark..."
