Chapter 1078: Turiyi's House Rental Experience

Later, some smart private homeowners went to discuss with the household registration department, and finally reached an agreement: these homeowners can also publish the information of the rental houses in the household registration office, and the household registration personnel will recommend it, but Prices are subject to the supervision of the Ministry of Commerce, taxes must be paid on time, and rental houses are subject to supervision by the construction agency. Although the homeowner has paid a certain price, with the endorsement of the Kingdom's public sector, it is naturally favored by those in need. Later, other homeowners had to join in, otherwise there would be no business, thus gradually standardizing the chaotic housing rental market in the kingdom.

Of course, the recommendation of public rental housing and private rental housing is a big gap, especially in Thurii. Corruption and bribery will inevitably occur, and it has been reported by the tribune many times. This aroused the anger of Davos, and ordered the Supervisory Minister to Sesta personally supervised the inspection and arrested a group of household registration officials to suppress this evil trend. To this day, this area is still the target of the tribunes.

The reason why the person at the household registration office was happy to see someone wanting to rent a private house was because once the rental was successful, some landlords would express their gratitude to him, such as treating him to a meal, sending a basket of fruit, etc., because This is so trivial that the supervisory authorities will hardly care about it.

The household registration officer recommended a better private rental house to him, but Maximus just turned a deaf ear. After all, he was literate, and he already had his own requirements for the rental house: first, the house should be large enough, because he has a family of four. secondly, the rent should be cheaper because he does not have enough savings and plans to use most of the money on his children's education in the future, because according to his understanding, it will take at least five or six years for him to become a citizen of Theonia. years, and during this period of time, children are not eligible to go to Theonia School. They can only go to privately built schools. Although the tuition is expensive and the quality is worse, he can't let his children We will waste this time.

Maximus read through all the densely packed rental information on the wall, and roughly identified five or six targets. Then he took out 10 Obol and bought a small piece of papyrus (the original price of a piece of paper was less than 10 Obol (but it includes the service fee of the household registration office), copied the address above, thanked the household registration officer, and then left the household registration office, taking his family with him to find the homeowner according to the address on the paper.

The first homeowner we found was a widow. Her husband was a soldier of the First Legion and died in Latium. She did not remarry and took care of her three children alone. She relied on the 30 acres of land on the Sybari Plain left by her husband while letting her Their slaves were responsible for farming and renting out houses, and they lived a pretty good life.

But when she saw that the person who came to rent the house was a Roman, she immediately refused to rent it because her husband died under a Roman spear, and she still harbored resentment for this.

Maximus had no choice but to leave.

The owner of the second house he found was a disabled man, but he was a soldier of the Second Legion. He was seriously injured in the war with the Romans and had his feet amputated. He could tell at a glance that Maximus was a Roman. , without giving him a chance to speak, he kicked him out of the house.

Maximus had been to the city of Turiyi several times before, but he always entered in a hurry and returned in a hurry after finishing his work. He did not get in touch with the residents of Turiyi as deeply as he did today. This time he was deeply involved. After experiencing the impact of that war on the Thurii people, many of them probably had a very bad impression of the Romans, which made Maximus increase his vigilance.

He finally found the third homeowner, a middle-aged man. From the awkward way he walked, Maximus judged that his left leg was somewhat disabled, which gave him a bad feeling.

Sure enough, as soon as the middle-aged man saw him, he said: "You must be a Roman!"

Maximus felt tight, but he had to say truthfully: "...Yes, I am a Roman, and I just got the Free, ready to live in Thurii, so I want to rent your house."

The landlord did not reply directly, but looked at him, and then continued with emotion: "Although many people think that Romans and Latins are the same race , they all look the same, so it's hard to tell them apart.
