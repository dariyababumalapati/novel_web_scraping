Due to the plundering behavior of the Spartan generals and their subsequent acquittal, Athens went to war with Sparta and formed an alliance with Thebes. Soon there were Chios, Byzantium, Rhodes, and Eubia. ...The city-states joined this new alliance, and this new alliance was named the Second Athenian League (after all, the alliance was mainly established with Athens, and most of the city-states that joined the alliance were city-states that had formed an alliance with Athens in recent years). It was separated from the Delian League established by Athens during the Loponnesian War. The League declared that the purpose of its alliance was to protect the independence of the Greek states and resist Spartan oppression.

In order to summon and hire more soldiers to fight against Sparta, the Athenian citizens' assembly also passed a resolution to resume the collection of property taxes. In order to collect taxes more quickly and easily, the Athenian government also reformed the collection method.

The newly-established Thebes government focused all its energy on military affairs. They relied on solid city defenses to withstand the first wave of attacks by the Spartan coalition.

With the coming of autumn, the soldiers of the Spartan coalition returned to their respective city-states to prepare for the autumn harvest. There were only less than 3,000 Spartan warriors left under the command of King Cleombrotus of Sparta. He was unwilling to The precious Spartan warriors were lost in the siege, so he temporarily retreated to Plataea.

The Thebes government seized this precious truce time, mobilized the entire city, surrounded the city of Thebes, dug trenches, built barriers... and took all measures to strengthen the city's defense to defend against the next attack by the Spartan coalition. At the same time, they also actively united with the nearby independent Boeotian city-states and actively assisted them in strengthening their defenses in order to block Sparta from the territory of Thebes.

At the same time, the Spartan government also summoned the citizens in the city to conduct military training every day. The person in charge was Epaminondas. His outstanding performance in the previous battles won the support of the people in the city.
