In addition to the military camp headquarters, the most important building here is the granary. In order to preserve food, engineers from the Construction Department have racked their brains to design this kind of public granary in Theonia: it is a narrow and long two-story building. Buildings with fewer and smaller windows can prevent food from spoiling due to high temperatures or rain and humidity. However, in order to ensure that the granary is cool and breathable, multiple vents are built around the bottom of the granary, and the floor of the first floor of the granary is laid above the vents. In this way, even if mice and other pests get into the house through the vents, they will not Unable to pass through the floor, enter the granary and harm the grain. In order to prevent the walls from cracking due to excessive compression of grain or other items accumulated in the cabin, buttresses are built outside the walls of the granary. There are also many large dry wooden boxes placed in the granary, which are used to store the raw material for the soldiers' staple food - flour. When the soldiers use it, they only need to open the outlet at the bottom of the wooden box, and the flour will flow out, thus making the flour Basically in a sealed state, its shelf life will naturally be longer.
