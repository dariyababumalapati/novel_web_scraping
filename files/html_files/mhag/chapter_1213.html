"The Theonian army landed on the coast of Peleus!!" Polydorus was shocked when he heard the news, and his uneasiness was finally confirmed. The Theonians caught him off guard by using the tactics of attacking from the east and attacking from the west.

At this moment, he knew very well: because the Theonian army landed on the coast of Mildorus with great fanfare, he mobilized citizen soldiers from surrounding city-states to come to support. Now the defense in the west is very empty. Once the Theonian army passes through the pass, Road, rushing into the northern plains, the consequences will be disastrous. Peleus can be captured, but the valley pass must be blocked!
Polydorus made up his mind and immediately issued an order: two thousand Philae militiamen were stationed at the coastal camp of Mildorus, and the rest of the troops immediately rushed to the valley pass camp.

Considering that the city of Larissa is more than 100 miles away from the coast of Mildorus, and the valley pass is even further away, it has taken a lot of time for the Larissa messenger to arrive here. I am afraid that the battle situation on Peleus' side has changed a lot. ... Just in case, Polydorus ordered the only 500 cavalry under his command to quickly reinforce the valley pass.

After all the orders were issued, Polydorus felt relieved. He led the main force of more than 10,000 troops to advance towards the valley pass at a fast marching speed.

When night fell, the Thessalian army had marched less than 40 miles, but Polydorus was worried about the battle ahead and did not dare to let the soldiers stop and rest. He just asked them to light torches and continue moving forward.

It didn't take long before Polydorus finally got the exact news from the front, and this news was brought back by the cavalry troops he sent before.

It turned out that the Thessalian cavalry rushed from the coast of Mildorus in the southeast to the valley pass due north, and did not encounter the Theonian army rushing to Larissa. When they arrived at the valley pass camp, they found The camp was already full of Theonian soldiers in black helmets. The surprised Thessalian cavalry dared to go forward and fight. They were also worried about the safety of Larissa, who was not far away, so they turned to the south to explore. As a result, I happened to see the Theonian army attacking the city of Larissa.

The Thessalian troops he led could not be the best. opponent.

It was precisely because of these thoughts that he decided not to act rashly, but to concentrate his forces to defend Philae, and then send people to notify Jason and request reinforcements while observing the enemy's movements and looking for fighter opportunities.
