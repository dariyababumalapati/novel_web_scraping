The city of Gela has a long history. It was only four or five years later than the founding of Syracuse. It was founded by colonists from Rhodes Island and Crete. The city was named after the Gela River in the territory.

Because the Jela River washed away a large amount of soil from the mountains, it formed a fertile river plain in the Jela territory. After decades of successful transformation of the river swamps by the Jela people, Jela became prosperous, and this At that time, the city of Agrigento had just been established, and the city of Syracuse was still limited to the island of Ortygia. Therefore, Hippocrates, the well-equipped tyrant of Gera a hundred years ago, and his successor Dilon launched an attack eastward. Dillon took a fancy to Syracuse's geographical location and moved there, leaving his Brother Hieron took over Gela and moved half of the population of Gela, where he was born, to Syracuse, thus creating the beginning of Syracuse's prosperity. After Dillon's death, Hieron moved to Syracuse... After that, the tyrants of Gela abandoned their original city one after another and made Syracuse their center of rule. Gela was also the strongest in Sicily. The large Greek city-states began to decline. But by this point, Gera had also established a fairly close relationship with Syracuse.
