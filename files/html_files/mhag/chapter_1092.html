Chapter 1092: Observations on the Craty River
Standing on the side of the boat, Haka could see the long, encircling white breakwater at the mouth of the Craty River from a distance, with countless ships passing through it, as if it were a busy beehive.

As the boat approached, I discovered that there were multiple water gates open on the breakwater.

"All cargo ships must pass through the water gate on this side and obtain the wooden sign of the dock before they can enter; and the water gate on that side of the breakwater is responsible for inspecting the cargo ships leaving and handing over the wooden sign of the dock. If there is no wooden sign, they will You will be fined. But large ships like ours do not need to pass through the breakwater gate, but go through the wide water channel in the middle and accept inspection by patrol boats..." the captain explained to Haka in detail.

"Why do we have to make the entire cargo ship inspection process so cumbersome? This will greatly delay the time for cargo ships coming to trade to enter the port for delivery!" A subordinate of Hakka pointed at the long queue of cargo ships outside the water gate in front and asked himself objection.

"It is much better to let them delay for a little time outside the water gate than to swarm in, causing congestion to the entire Kraty River estuary and wasting more time. You can see that there are many boats outside the water gate. In fact, there are even more boats in the estuary. If there is no effective management, it will be a disaster!" the ship owner said seriously.

"You are right." Haka nodded in agreement.

"The owner directed the large passenger ship to bypass the long queue of boats in front of the breakwater and enter the middle river. There

He asked the ship owner a few questions and checked the documents submitted by the ship owner. Then he looked surprised and jumped immediately. Boarding the passenger ship, he strode up to Haka and saluted respectfully: "Salute to you, Lord Haka! "
