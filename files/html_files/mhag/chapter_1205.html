This army was mainly drawn from the Thessaly and Athenian armies, mainly composed of Illyrian peltasts and Thracian peltasts, and was personally led by Iphicrates, preparing to march from the northeastern border of Sparta. Iphicrates decisively ordered a retreat, but the Theonian army quickly launched a pursuit. Although the mountain people of Illyria and Thrace were good at running in the mountains, there were still many of them who were exhausted. They were overtaken by the recharged Theonian light armor. As a result, nearly 1,000 Greek coalition forces were captured before the battle was fought. Even Iphicrates himself felt frustrated.

The Greek coalition generals had to discuss again, and finally decided to shift the direction of the attack and concentrate their efforts on attacking Messenia, because the terrain in its north is relatively flat and wide compared to the northern border of Sparta.
