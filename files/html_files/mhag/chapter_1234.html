Chapter 1234 The Establishment of the New Hellenic Alliance (2)

On January 5, the New Hellenic Alliance meeting was officially held. It was chaired by Clotokatax and assisted by Xiros. Nearly 200 city-state envoys participated in the meeting. In the large alliance Even though the framework of the agreement had been established, the envoys still had heated debates over some small details.

On January 11, the final alliance agreement was established. Its main contents are:
First, Article 1 is that Theonia, Athens, Sparta, Thebes, Argos... The Greek city-states voluntarily established New Greece Alliance, and voluntarily elected Theonia as the permanent leader of the alliance;
after the establishment of the New Hellenic Alliance Parliament, each city-state recommended a citizen of the city-state to become an alliance member, which was changed every three years, but the members of Theonia He will always serve as the chairman of the alliance;

members within the alliance are not allowed to launch wars with each other, otherwise the alliance will form an army, led by Theonia, to carry out crusade against the initiators of the war;
conflicts among members within the alliance must be submitted to the alliance parliament, which will be decided by Theonia is responsible for forming an investigation team to discuss and resolve the issue;
if members of the alliance are invaded by foreign enemies, the alliance will form an army, led by Theonia, until the foreign enemies are repelled to ensure the safety of all members of the alliance;

members of the alliance If you want to launch a foreign war, you can make a request to the Alliance Parliament. If the alliance discussion passes, it will provide help. If it does not pass, it will forcefully launch a war. The alliance will not guarantee the security of its territory, and Theonia has a If any other city-state wants to join the alliance, it
must be discussed and approved by the parliament, but Theonia has a veto power;

Then a famous Greek engraver engraved the covenant and its signatures on a copper pillar three meters high and one meter in diameter. Then the envoys from each city-state signed the covenant. On March 1, a grand triumphal ceremony was held in Thurii, the capital of the Sacred Kingdom of Theonia.

When the news of the "conquest of mainland Greece" spread to the territory of Theonia, the people of Greek origin were ecstatic. You must know that most of them are either refugees from the Eastern Mediterranean or descendants of immigrants from the mainland Greek city-states. Even though the country is now rich and the people are strong, there is still a hint of colonial humility deep in the heart, and now those Greek city-states that they once regarded as their mother-state have surrendered to Theonia, so the pride of being proud is beyond words. They scattered flowers, sang and danced to welcome the soldiers who had been waiting for this triumphal ceremony for several months.

On the platform in front of the Great Council Hall, Davos was as happy as the people, especially when he saw his son striding up, his eyes never stopped looking at him.

When Clotokatax came close to him, he "bang" his feet, gave an unusually powerful military salute, and shouted in a loud voice: "Your Majesty, Commander of the Greek Expeditionary Force, Clotokatax" Si, salute to you! "

At that moment, Hades Davos, who had experienced countless storms and had been in office for more than 20 years, was a little excited. His voice was slightly trembling and he said: "My child... you have lost a lot of weight, but you look more energetic! You did a great job and lived up to my expectations of you! "

My father's kind words melted away the perseverance that Crotokatax had cultivated during more than half a year of fighting. He suddenly had red circles in his eyes and whispered guiltily: "Father, please forgive me, I didn't take good care of my eldest brother! "

Davos comforted him softly: "This is not your fault! Your mother and I did not blame you, and neither did Cynthia and Aidui! After the triumphal ceremony is over, we will go home together to Adoris's grave. Come and worship!"

He patted Crotokatax hard on the shoulder, handed the golden scepter into his hand, and said encouragingly: "But now you must cheer up and celebrate with the people of the kingdom. This great victory was bought with the blood of countless soldiers!"

Crotokatax nodded, tried his best to smile, and turned with Davos to face the thousands of soldiers and people in the square, standing tall He raised his scepter.

The cheers suddenly rose higher and higher like a raging ocean tide.

A month later, Davos appointed Clotokatax as the consul of the Holy Kingdom of Theonia, and he worked with the six auxiliary ministers to handle state affairs.

Attica

in Athens There is Mount Aigaleus in the territory, with the city of Athens to the east and a small town called Selesia at the foot of the mountain to the west. Because of the high-quality pottery clay on the nearby river bank, it is famous throughout Greece for its pottery industry.

There are many pottery houses and kilns in the town. Every day, countless smoke pillars rise here, day and night, shrouding the entire town in smoke and dust. Outsiders often feel worried for the town residents when they see this situation, but at this time the Athenians will calmly tell them: This is a good thing, it means the prosperity of the Athenian pottery industry!

In fact, this is indeed the case. Even after the New Hellenic Alliance was established and Theonia became the new overlord of Greece, the worries of the Athenian pottery merchants did not appear. Not only did the Athenian pottery industry not decline, but the orders were far greater than before.

Early in the morning, Heskraus set out from the suburbs of Athens with a pack team and rushed to Thracia.

A large house near the town gate in the west area of ​​the town was his pottery yard. As soon as he entered the yard, the foreman in charge of the entire pottery factory immediately came to report to him: the last batch of pottery was about to leave the kiln.

He immediately rushed to the kiln. The kiln had just been opened. In the hot air filled with black ash, pieces of red-burned pottery were displayed in front of everyone's eyes: their shapes were various, but they were all different from traditional ones. Athenian pottery style.

Everyone present was accustomed to this, because these pottery was customized by the buyer, and these styles were also provided by the buyer, who was a Turiyi merchant.

You must know that when Heskraus initially handed over the new patterns provided by the buyers to the potters who were responsible for making embryos and shapes, these skilled Athenian potters refused to make them, thinking that these styles were deviant and random designs. For this reason, Heskraus fired potters one after another. The potters' collective boycott once put his just-started pottery business into dire straits. But later, more than half of the pottery fields in Thracia received pottery from Theonia. orders, and the price was considerable, the potters who needed to be clothed and fed finally chose to give in.

While waiting for the temperature of the pottery to cool down, Heskraus went to the vase painting room next to him. He wanted to express his gratitude to the painter Onesimus and his assistants who painted the patterns for these pottery works, and to give him generous remuneration. .
