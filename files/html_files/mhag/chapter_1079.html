Chapter 1079 Street Tavern
In fact, in the traditional concept of the Greeks, it is not Hades's priesthood to be in charge of rivers and springs. However, since he performed miracles in Tuli Spring and brought him back to life, the Theonians gradually believed that Hades He was the real protector of springs and drinking water, and Poseidon became a supporter.

At this time, there were several women carrying multiple earthen jars by the pool, chatting and drinking water. The children were chasing and playing around the pool and the surrounding stone pillars.

Asimus's two children were eager to try and join in, but Fulvia stopped them.

Although Maximus was surprised and confused as to how the water in the pool came out, he did not ask. Instead, he followed the slave, climbed the stairs to the third floor, and walked along the somewhat dark corridor to the fortress. Rooms for rent.

I met several tenants along the way, and they all greeted the servant humbly. Apparently, the landlord usually asked this servant to take care of the entire rental building.

Although the room was simple, with only a bed, a table, and a few chairs, it was clean, spacious, and had good lighting. It was much better than the house he lived in in the slave camp, so Maxim immediately decided to rent it.

After placing the package in the room, he went to the tax office with the slave and signed a rental agreement.

Finally, the rental matter was settled. It was already dusk. Maximus and his wife felt a little hungry, and the two children lost the energy to play, so Maximus decided to take the whole family to eat something. ,Fill the stomach.

There are many restaurants near the place where they rented, among which Christoya's Restaurant is the most famous. It is full of customers early and there are people lining up outside the door.

However, considering the price issue, Maximus finally chose to eat at a cheaper street pub.

This kind of street-facing pub usually only has a circular counter. There are multiple circular pits in the stone counter. Open wine cans, beer cans and clay pots are embedded in these pits. They are also inlaid on the stone wall at the end of the counter. There is a shrine, but usually it is not Hades, but Dionysus, the Greek god of wine.

When the Maximus family walked into the street tavern, there were already some diners inside, making the small store seem a bit crowded.

The cook in the middle of the counter saw them, and immediately looked over with a smile and asked: "What do you want to eat and drink?"

"Is there anything I can fill my stomach with?” Maximus asked, looking at the bottles and cans on the counter.

"Bread, cheese, vegetables, nuts and olives... can be provided to you right away. Of course, if you need some hot soup, we can also cook haggis soup and beef soup, wheat porridge and Bean porridge, but this will take some time." The cook looked at the Maximus family, estimated their needs, and said fluently: "Of course, the most important thing here is that we can provide you with all kinds of products produced in the kingdom. Wine and beer, and relatively cheap.”

"Bring me four pieces of bread, four slices of cheese, two bowls of porridge, and a large bowl of haggis soup." Maximus thought for a while and said.

"Don't you drink some wine?” The cook tried her best to persuade: "It seems that you have walked a long way. You must be tired and thirsty. Why not drink a glass of beer to moisten your throat and relieve your fatigue.”

Maximus was not someone who could be easily persuaded, but when he saw the diners holding wine glasses and talking loudly at the counter, he suddenly changed his mind: "Bring me a beer."

"Which beer do you want to drink?" The cook pointed to a round pit on the counter in front of her and said enthusiastically: "I recommend Kristoya Beer to you. This is a brewery run by Princess Kristoya. It was they who invented Theonia's beer, so their beer is the most authentic, best-tasting, and very refreshing. It is very popular with the people of the kingdom. Because of the high demand, most of the output is Those damn rich businessmen and officials bought and stored them for banquets and entertaining guests, but there are not many on the market. Our tavern got some because of a special relationship. Although the price is a little more expensive, it is very rare."

Even though Maximus was a little moved by what she said, he still suppressed his desire to speak and said, "Just give me a glass of ordinary beer."

Although the cook's persuasion failed, she did not show any frustration and said to him with a smile: "Okay. Then one loaf of bread is 5 opors, four loaves is 20 opos... a total of 67 opos. Well, it's converted into nine drachmas and three opors. You pay the money first, and I'll prepare food for you right away.”

Well, the price of this dinner is close to one month's rent! In the past, Maximus wouldn't have cared about this small amount of money, but more than three years of slave life made him deeply aware of the hardships of life. He took a breath, counted 10 drachmas from the money bag, and retrieved 4 opoles.

The cook looked at the crowd, and then shouted loudly: "Hey! There are children who want to eat, please take the initiative to give up your seat!"

As soon as her shrill voice shouted, someone actually stood up from the bench against the wall. Fulvia quickly squeezed past with her children, thanked the diners, and then sat down.

Maximus stood at the counter.

After a while, the cook put the bread and cheese he ordered into a small wooden basket and handed them over.

Maximus felt that the bread was still hot and couldn't help but secretly nodded at the tavern's attentiveness.

The cook opened a wooden lid, reached into the clay pot in the stone pit with a slender wooden spoon, scooped up the beer, poured a full glass, and handed it to Maximus. As for the wheat porridge and mutton soup, there were still some. It takes time to cook.

Maximus took the beer and took a sip first, feeling that his dry throat was moisturized immediately. In fact, there was no beer in Rome before. Maximus started to like drinking this cheap and soothing drink in the slave camp.

The family sat in the corner and began to eat. Maximos was chewing bread while listening carefully to the conversations of the diners.
