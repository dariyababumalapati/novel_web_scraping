Chapter 1219 Jason's Return

According to Greek tradition, after a battle, the defeated party can also send people to clean the battlefield and take away the compatriots who died in the battle in order to give them a decent burial. In the past, Theonia rarely fulfilled this tradition, because they were the victors most of the time, and in order to expand the results, they were good at continuing to pursue after the victory. The opponent was afraid of not being able to escape, so there was no way to think about other things. Only during this period of time did the Theonian army defend against the attack of the Greek coalition forces, and this Greek military tradition could be well fulfilled.

Tens of thousands of physically and mentally exhausted Greek coalition soldiers began to slowly retreat toward Leprion's camp.

Leprion is less than 20 miles away from the northern border of Messenia. This small city located in the southern part of the Elis region was originally attached to Elis, but was later taken away by Sparta. After Elis joined the Greek League, it became confident again to take it back. Since the Greek coalition forces were frequently harassed by Theonian soldiers when they attacked the Lagnia area, after moving their troops to attack Messenia, Iphicrates learned his lesson and stationed his army in Leprion, who was far away from the battlefield, was mainly for safety.
