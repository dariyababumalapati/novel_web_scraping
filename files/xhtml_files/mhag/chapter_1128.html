Now, the final decision rests with five inspectors.

The Spartan censors had great power. They could review and supervise the king and elders internally, and they were responsible for diplomacy and supervising the troops abroad. However, they were selected from ordinary civilians who had been educated since childhood. They underwent arduous military training and continued to fight as adults. The reason is very simple: the successive wars with Thebes have not made any significant progress, but the country is becoming increasingly difficult. Soldiers continued to suffer casualties, the people were miserable, and war weariness was very serious. The choices of the censors reflected the public opinion at this time. The Spartan people were eager to stop the war and restore peace. As for the possibility that this will lead to a more powerful enemy Theonia, they will not consider that far.
