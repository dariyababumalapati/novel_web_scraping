Moreover, Leotychides took into account that there were many older citizens among Jela's soldiers (the Jela people came out in full force this time to defend their homeland), and deliberately thickened the array to prevent it from being The enemy easily breaks through the front.

At the same time, considering the weakness of the Greek heavy infantry-the flanks were attacked and easily routed, Leotychides moved the left wing close to the coast, thus preventing the Carthaginians from detouring from the left and attacking the flanks of friendly forces.

In addition, another purpose of thickening the array was to shorten the length of the battle array, because although the land to the west of Jela is flat, it is not wide. There are only 7-8 miles from the coast to the mountains. Leotizide Si needs to leave enough space outside the right wing for the cavalry to gallop, because he has determined the victory of the Theonian coalition on the right wing, because there is his trusted 8th Army here, and he can go around to the opposite side at any time. After thinking carefully, he figured out the reason why he dared to fight the Carthaginians so quickly. The cavalry was his biggest reliance.

Leoticides and his adjutant Muklu stood in front of the military formation on horseback, watching the Carthaginian formation on the opposite side. There were also scouts who reported to him the entire formation of the Carthaginian army at any time.

Although the Theonian intelligence department did not collect much intelligence on the Carthaginian army in the Iberia region (this is because Carthage, in order to protect the source of its precious metal mines, had entered into a previous peace agreement with Theonia It clearly stated the clause that "Theonian merchant ships are not allowed to trade in the Carthage territory of Iberia". After roughly understanding the formation of the Carthaginian army, Leotychides breathed a sigh of relief, turned to Muklu and said: "It seems that the Carthaginians are indeed worried about the attack power of our legionnaires, so they specially sent Their main force in Iberia was placed on the left wing. In this way...we formed the formation first and used the Eighth Army to attract the opponent's main force and reduce the pressure on friendly soldiers."

The reason why he said this was because Muklu reminded him before: It is best not to set up the formation before the enemy arrives, because it was mentioned in the "Theonian Military Law" that it is best not to let go before the battle. The enemy needs to know his own array deployment first to avoid having the opportunity to make targeted arrangements.

At this time, Muklu did not blindly agree. He reminded: "Commander, the Carthaginians have arranged those Lusitanians on their right wing!"

"It used to be rumored that the Messapians were very fierce, the Lucanians were very fierce, the Samnites were very fierce... but they did not pose much of a threat to Theonia, and now it is rumored that the Lusitanians are very fierce. , I think we shouldn't blindly believe these rumors, let's take a look at the battle situation first." There was a hint of disdain in Leotychides' calm tone, and he in turn reminded Muklu: "The largest Greek heavy infantry The advantage is that their frontal defense is very strong and their attack power is not weak. This is exactly where our coalition forces are superior to their opponents except for the cavalry, so as long as it is a frontal fight, we will not be at a disadvantage."

At this time, the messenger of the Eighth Army arrived and reported to him that the army had completed its formation.

Leotychides immediately looked back to the left. The noise continued, and he was somewhat dissatisfied: "Immediately send someone to urge the Gera and Camanlina people to speed up the formation!"

After speaking, he turned his horse's head, passed through the gap in the military formation, and returned to the rear of the center.

"Woo!..." More than ten minutes later, the Theonian coalition blew the horn of attack.

The entire military formation began to move forward slowly, and the fastest was the light infantry brigade of the Eighth Army. They passed through the gaps in the checkerboard military formation of the Eighth Army and rushed towards the enemy on the opposite side in a loose formation. They wanted to Use bows, arrows and javelins to deal the first wave of blows to the enemy, disrupt the enemy's formation, and pave the way for victory in the subsequent heavy infantry attack.

Hasdrubal and Kisgo knew about Theonia's past battles, and naturally knew Theonia's heavy infantry tactics. When they saw the black-helmeted and black-armored Eighth Legion Opposite their left wing, they were on guard, so when the Eighth Army's light infantry group rushed out of the military formation, Kisgo also dispatched the troops that had been waiting behind the left wing.

There are about 1,700 people in this army, 500 of whom are Lusitanian harpooneers wearing black robes. They hold a hooked javelin. After this javelin pierces the human body, it is difficult to pull it out. Therefore, the Lusitanian harpooneer is the most talked about in Iberia.

The 1,200 soldiers following them were wearing white singlets, with a heavy cloth bag tied around their waists and a dagger hanging on them. They were not wearing helmets or armor, and they were even barefoot. They just held a knife in their hands. A sling woven from a mixture of wool and linen... Yes, they were slingers, slingers from the Balearic Islands.

The Balearic Islands are a series of islands in the Western Mediterranean near the east coast of the Iberian Peninsula. The residents of the islands have always had a tradition of training slingers. Children are trained as soldiers from an early age. Their mothers even started No bread is given to the child until he hits a distant target with his first sling. The Balearic people, who lack resources and have poor supplies, use a large number of cheap slingers to defend the security of the island. Residents with superb slinging skills often use this skill to obtain meat and improve their lives, such as using slinging to kill birds.

Mago occupied the southern part of Iberia (mainly the southeast), which happened to be adjacent to the Balearic Islands. He obtained the allegiance of the Balearic people by providing sufficient food for the Balearic people. In the process of conquering Iberia , the Balearic slingers played a big role. This time during the expedition to Sicily, Mago brought 1,500 Balearic slingers.

After the Balearic people walked out of the military formation, they stopped advancing. They put their hands into the cloth bags on their waists. The cloth bags were divided into three compartments, and three types of lead shot were placed respectively. They took out the lightest lead shot. Place it in the sling's pocket and start swinging it.

Since the Theonian coalition had just begun to advance, after the Eighth Army light infantry brigade rushed out of the military formation, it was still more than 400 meters away from the enemy on the opposite side. Therefore, they were advancing rapidly, and the formation had not completely dispersed. Suddenly, they saw diagonal A dark cloud flew in the sky ahead.

"Be careful of flying rocks! Protect your head!" the light infantry captain shouted, his face changing a bit.

At the reminder of the team officers, the light infantrymen who had never been attacked by slingers lowered their heads and hugged their necks with their hands. Although the Theonian light infantry wore single clothes, they were all equipped with a leather helmet. After the lead shot fell, there was a roar, but it did not cause many casualties.
