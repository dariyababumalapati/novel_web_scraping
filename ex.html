    Chapter 1 Reinforcements

    In the center of the western Mediterranean there is a large island of Sicily, which is shaped like a triangle. Its northeast corner is close to the Apennine Peninsula, its southwest corner overlooks Carthage, and its southeast corner is located at the boundary between deep and shallow seas. There is a city-state called Syracuse nearby.

    On this day, the sea water was crystal blue, like a huge piece of jade without a trace of impurities. The waves beat rhythmically against the rocks, as if singing a soothing ballad. Countless seabirds circled and soared in the clear sky, and their loud cries seemed to echo the ancient tune of the Mediterranean.

    In the past, Latmans would breathe the moist air full of seawater and happily guide the merchant ships entering the harbor. At that time, his family had several acres of fertile fields, and his wife was good at managing the household, so the harvest was good. As a lighthouse keeper, he could also receive a monthly salary of two drachmas from the council.

    But since the Athenian army attacked Syracuse, his life changed completely. He had to lead his family to hide in the city, leaving the fields and houses to the Athenians to ravage. Every day, he lined up in a long line to go to the square to get relief food, and slept in crowded and dirty alleys at night. His eldest son was stabbed to death by the spear of the Athenian heavy infantry in the first battle. His second son fell from the top of the wall during the assault to destroy the long wall built by the Athenians to besiege Syracuse and became a cripple. ... Latomans' wife couldn't face this reality and went crazy.

    Whenever he thought of this, Latomans would clench his fists, tears would well up in his eyes, and he wished he could put on a bronze breastplate, pick up a spear, and fight these evil Athenians to the death. Unfortunately, he was already old.

    Fortunately, the situation is starting to improve. Under the pressure of the Athenian army, many city-states in Sicily felt threatened. They united and sent reinforcements to Syracuse. Corinth, the mother state of Syracuse, and Sparta, the overlord of the Peloponnese, also sent a small number of troops and ships, as well as officers with rich combat experience... After nearly a year of fighting, the coalition forces captured Primmilion and surrounded the Athenian fleet in the harbor. Just yesterday, another naval battle was launched, and the Athenian fleet was defeated by strategy, thus breaking the myth of the invincibility of the Athenian navy.

    "We will launch a second attack soon!" Latomans thought anxiously. He looked to the south. From this position, he could only vaguely see the Athenian camp, but in his mind he imagined the Athenian sailors repairing the warships with their heads down. He even thought of buying a few Athenian prisoners after the war was won and beating them up every day to relieve his hatred.

    Suddenly, several loud bird calls caught his attention. The seabirds that were foraging to the north of the lighthouse flew high in the sky in panic. A sail appeared on the calm sea. Dozens of triremes soon came into Rathmans' sight.

    "Athenian reinforcements!" Latomans' heart suddenly tightened.

    …

    "Oh, I saw Syracuse!" Hilo shouted excitedly on the troop transport.

    The other soldiers on the same ship were equally excited. Most of them were veterans who had fought in the Aegean Sea for a long time, and the farthest they had fought was around the southern tip of the Peloponnese Peninsula to attack Sparta. However, this was the first time they had crossed the Greek mainland and attacked the city-states in the Greater Greece region.

    Everyone rushed to the shore side of the boat, pointing at the towering city walls on the steep coast. The boat began to shake due to uneven force.

    "You brainless idiot, get back to where you were!" Along with cursing, a sturdy middle-aged man swung a stick and hit the soldiers who were gathered together.

    These veterans were obviously very familiar with the man. They ignored his anger and returned to their original positions slowly. From time to time, someone would joke, "Nurnusis, why are you so angry? You must have been holding back for too long at sea and are not satisfied with your sexual desires."

    Nurnusis frowned at the laughter. He stood at the bow, like a towering mountain, with an imposing manner: "I am the boss on this ship. If anyone doesn't follow the rules, I will throw him into the sea to avoid getting everyone involved."

    There was silence on the boat.

    Nurnusius knew that these soldiers were extremely dissatisfied, but he didn't care. The conflict between the Athenian heavy infantry and the Athenian sailors has a long history. The most powerful military force in Greece is the heavy infantry, which was confirmed in the Persian War. Most of the heavy infantry were wealthy citizens who owned land in the city-state, while the sailors were poor people without land, and the status of the two was very different. However, the situation in Athens was different, because Athens' prosperity came from the strength of the Athenian navy, and the status of Athenian sailors was greatly improved. This disparity in economic status and the difference in political and military importance made the two armies always have some minor frictions when they were together.

    "Uncle Noor, have you been to Syracuse before?" a voice sounded at this time.

    Nurnousis looked in the direction of the sound and saw that it was Hero. Hero was different from others, as his family was a famous wealthy businessman in Athens. Normally, Nurnousis relied on helping Hat to transport goods and earn money to support himself and his dozens of brothers.

    As the saying goes, eating someone else's food will make you soft-hearted. Nurnussis was somewhat helpless: "I have been there. Syracuse is a wealthy city-state, and a large city-state. It has great influence in Sicily."

    "No matter how influential it is, we Athenians will destroy it like crushing an ant!" one of the soldiers shouted.

    6,000 heavy infantry and nearly 40,000 combat troops. Athens had never concentrated such a large number of troops before, and their confidence was unprecedentedly high.

    "Why destroy them? We want to rob them all!" Someone else shouted, and everyone laughed knowingly. Occupying land, capturing people, and robbing wealth were the main reasons why they came here from thousands of miles away.

    Nurnus saw Shiloh shouting like everyone else and sighed secretly. It was well known that Shiloh loved fighting. But he didn't expect that Shiloh, who was only 18 years old, would rush to join the expedition without two years of reserve service. Could this young man who only had martial arts and no business talent support his huge family business?

    "Captain, there's a signal coming from the front, we're about to enter the port!"

    "Everyone, take your positions!" Nurnusis looked ahead and shouted solemnly, "Lower the sails!"

    "Lower the sails!!"

    "Paddle!"

    "Paddle up!!"

    More than a hundred ships formed a huge arrow shape, with three rows of oared warships on the outside, surrounding the transport ships in the center.

    Thousands of oars were paddling rapidly on the sea at the same time, stirring up waves. The sounds of horns and splashing water came one after another, which was truly spectacular.

    The soldiers stood on both sides of the ship, and they could even see the Syracuse soldiers on the coast beating their shields and cursing loudly. At this time, it was useless for them to be anxious, and everything depended on the performance of the Athenian navy.

    "I begin to sing about bright-eyed Pallas Athena, defender of the city..." Someone began to hum the hymn to Athena, and gradually the singing spread to the entire fleet.

    "She is fear, and like Ares she loves war,

    The plundered city was filled with the cries of war.

    Those who went out to fight were saved by her and returned.

    Oh! Goddess, you give us blessings and joy! "

    The passionate and powerful voice hovered over the rough sea, as if the Syracuse people were intimidated and did not send a fleet to block the attack.

    The Athenian fleet sailed into the harbor in a mighty manner.
