Chapter 11 Take a casual trip

Novel: The King of German Mercenaries Author: Taishang Lao Niu
　　Marin lay on the bed of the Vienna hotel and thought about what he would do in the next few months. Vienna is very expensive, a clean hotel, the accommodation fee for three people includes the cost of parking animals, which costs 40 pfennigs a day. The food money for the three of Marin and Makar is as high as 40 pfennigs a day. Even if two cows eat grass, they will still be charged. After all, this is Vienna, not a country town, and there is no grass to eat everywhere. You have to ask the helper to get the grass, and there is a fee.

　　The consumption of more than 80 pfennigs a day made Marin feel a little overwhelmed. So, Marin decided to leave Vienna temporarily and go to a place with lower consumption. It would be even more perfect if I could go hunting and make some money...

　　After making up his mind, Marin asked Kahn and Kohler to start packing their luggage the next morning. After packing, Marin went to the innkeeper to pay the bill. Then, he took two attendants and had a walk-off meeting. travel....

　　As for where to go, Marin has already thought about it - they will return to the Black Forest...

　　There are many animal resources in the Black Forest, and Marin's archery skills are very good. With Kahn's cooperation, they can make a fortune from hunting. Moreover, it seems that the lords near the Black Forest are not strict about poaching, which is very suitable for Marin to "commit crimes".

　　Marin's plan was to first raise a large amount of money through hunting. With money, you can change the equipment later and equip yourself, Kahn and Kohler. Also, after the Italian war is over, I can buy myself a small manor and become the owner of the manor.

　　As for status, you still have to rely on fighting with the emperor. He knew the outcome of the war. In the First Italian War, the Shinra Empire was considered the victor, and there was a lot of military merit to be gained. As long as he can save his life on the battlefield and use his foresight to gain some military merit, he can at least exchange the lowest noble status of a formal knight with the emperor. If you're lucky, you might be able to become a baron or something. If we could take advantage of the chaos and catch the escaping Charles VIII, that would be a great achievement...

　　Also, Marin needs to recruit 3 more knight followers. The average poor knight also has 5 or more knight attendants. I only have two, which makes me look particularly shabby.

　　But raising five knights and squires is a big expense. Some knights are of very bad character and will block roads and rob when they have no money. But Marin is a moral person and would not do such an immoral thing. Therefore, he decided to save enough money by hunting first. After saving enough money, he not only had to recruit five attendants, but also bought each attendant a packhorse, equipped with leather armor, spears and other weapons and equipment.

　　He thought about it and found that Kahn could be considered a good combat force, but Kohler was a bit thin and could only play roles such as quartermaster and dog-headed strategist. Then, if you want to recruit three more people, it is naturally best to recruit those who can fight and be loyal.

　　The squire is the knight's most trusted partner and subordinate. Therefore, in addition to ability, reliability is the most important. The reason why Marin chose Kahn and Kohler was because he liked Kahn's honesty and honesty. As for Kohler, it's actually a sideshow. Moreover, Marin knows where the Kahn family is. If they dare to betray, he can go to their house to settle the score.

　　Nowadays, the economy in Germany is very poor and there are many refugees. But among these refugees, Marin did not dare to recruit followers casually. Because the origins of most of the refugees are unknown, which is not so reassuring. It's okay to recruit them as ordinary soldiers, but it's not appropriate to recruit them as servants. You know, knights basically leave their backs to their attendants, and nothing can be done carelessly.

　　After much thought, Marin decided to go back to Hoffman Manor and recruit servants from the serf families of Hoffman Manor. There were more than 20 households of serfs in Hoffman Estates, and Marin "remembered" that there were still some good guys here. His "eldest brother" Adler Hoffman had already selected five knights and attendants. And there are actually quite a few good guys in the manor. After all, these serf families did not each have only one child. Some families have several children. Because of the insufficient output of the estate, some families with many children even had to send their children to the city to work as coolies. Otherwise, Hoffman Estates wouldn't be able to feed so many people. After all, Hoffman Estates only has 1,200 acres of land.

　　Of course, before going home, Marin felt that it was right to save more money by hunting. In fact, the elder Hoffman is also worried about the future armor and horses of his third and fourth sons. After all, as a father, when his son is old, even if he is driven out of the house, he must prepare horses, armor and lances for him. In addition, there will be a large amount of travel expenses.

　　Marin's plan was to subsidize the Hoffmans some money after returning home so that the economic situation of the manor would be better. By the way, he also wanted to give some advice on his family's agricultural techniques so that the manor's land could produce more.

　　The financial situation of the manor has improved, and his cheaper brothers Simon and Albert can also be equipped with horses, armor and weapons when they were kicked out of the family. To avoid being like myself, who didn't even have plate armor and trousers when leaving home, let alone an attendant. Moreover, with sufficient funds, it will be easier for his cheap sister Anne to get married. After all, the more dowry, the easier it is to marry into a good family.

　　In order to bring enough money home, Marin took Kahn and Kohler and plunged into the black forest again and began a crazy hunting...

　　The Black Forest is huge, much larger than the Black Forest in later generations. After all, Germany was highly developed in later generations. In the Middle Ages, due to the limited population and many undeveloped areas, the Black Forest still maintained a very large area.

　　In order not to get lost, Marin bought a compass to avoid getting lost in the lush black forest. Spring is also a season when all things come to life, and animals in the forest are very active. There are also many adult animals doing the shameful thing of "carrying on the family line" in the forest in broad daylight. In this case, the wretched Marin would not bless them, but give them two arrows. Then, a pair of miserable "mandarin ducks" became Marin's trophies, and then were exchanged for a lot of money...

　　The Black Forest in the Middle Ages was too big and contained too many prey. This also caused some trouble for the three Marin people to transport the prey back to the forest-side town. As a last resort, Marin untied the big black bull "Li Chen", put a saddle on it, and let it enter the forest to carry prey. Because a four-wheeled ox cart cannot enter the depths of the forest where there are few roads.

　　In some places, because the Marin trio hunted too many prey, the price of meat even dropped. At the most, Marin hunted 6 red deer from the forest in one day. As a result, the price of local venison dropped from 5 pfennigs per pound (retail price) to 4 pfennigs per pound (purchase price) in a short period of time. It also dropped from 4 pfennig to 3 pfennig. Of course, some time after the Marin trio left, the price of venison here returned to normal.

　　For this reason, some local lords sent people to drive away Marin and his party. Because, in the eyes of those lords, the prey that Marin hunted belonged to their private property. However, the lords who were entrusted to the edge of the Black Forest were generally small lords. The territories of powerful lords were mostly in areas with developed farming. Only small lords who are not strong will be entrusted to the Black Forest area. And these little lords generally don't dare to offend wandering knights easily. Even if Marin was hunting wildly, they just sent people to drive them away. And Marin is also very conscious. Basically, after staying in a certain area for a day or two, he sells his prey and immediately escapes without giving others a chance to catch him on the spot. Communication was backward in the Middle Ages. By the time the local lord learned that Marin and his party had hunted a large number of prey and sent people to "enforce the law," the three of Marin had already left.

　　On average, the three people in Marin can get two to three red deer, as well as varying numbers of hares and foxes every day. On the other hand, Marin didn't dare to mess with the wild boar in the forest. Because he was not sure that he could shoot through the hard hair and thick skin of the wild boar with a 60-pound bow. As for Kahn being able to defeat the wild boar, Marin thought that was accidental. Because last time the wild boar was not heading for Kahn, but for the war horse. Kahn took advantage of the wild boar's side and killed it unprepared. Unless Kahn can wear a full set of plate armor, he can more confidently challenge the wild boar head-on. But having a follower wear a full set of plate armor is too extravagant. As for Marin's plate armor, UUkanshuwww.uukanshu. NET Kahn can't put it on again. Because Kahn is much taller than Marin.

　　The three of them were leisurely hunting and making money along the way, enjoying the beautiful scenery of the black forest, and feeling at ease. When he entered the territory of Uncle Mark, after taking inventory, Marin was surprised to find that his personal assets had reached more than 500 marks. This data is almost close to the annual income of Hoffman Estates...

　　"Hunting is so promising!" Marin sighed with emotion.

　　However, Marin also knew very well that the reason why he was able to successfully get back the assets of more than 500 marks was because others valued his status as an untitled knight. Otherwise, ordinary hunters would be extorted by the soldiers guarding the city when entering and leaving the city, and most of the profits would be confiscated by the lord. Even if they could hunt prey worth 500 marks, at least more than 400 marks would be extorted by lords, tax officials, and soldiers guarding the city.

　　Moreover, it is impossible for ordinary hunters to have horses. This means that hunters on foot cannot penetrate too deeply into the black forest. If you stay in the black forest overnight, you will basically be eaten by wolves as a midnight snack. Not only that, they don't have the shooting skills of Marin or the cooperation of a best partner like Kahn.

　　Therefore, Marin's success is difficult for ordinary Orions to copy. And people who truly have the status of knights generally don't practice archery. They are usually used to hand-to-hand combat, and few are good at archery... Besides, who among those with status would put aside their status to become a hunter?

　　Even Marin himself believes that hunting is just a stopgap measure. If a time-traveler makes a living by hunting, that would be the greatest shame in the time-travel world...
