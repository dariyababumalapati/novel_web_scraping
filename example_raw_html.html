Chapter 1 The unlucky wandering knight

Novel: The King of German Mercenaries Author: Taishang Lao Niu
Marin was very depressed. He was playing "European Universal Universal" at home, and he finally became the Holy Roman Emperor. He was getting high, but his friend Li Liang called him for help and asked him to help fight.

As for the cause of the incident, it was because Li Liang went out to practice his stall at night and had a conflict with a group of gangsters over a dispute over the stall's territory. Then, Li Liang was beaten.

Li Liang was naturally dissatisfied, so he called a few friends to get back on his feet. Marin is tall and agile, and he learned military grappling from a veteran when he was a child. Naturally, he was the "mythical beast" summoned by Li Liang first.

Marin had just swept the entire Holy Roman Empire in the game and became the emperor worshiped by thousands of people. At this moment, his blood was boiling, so without saying a word, he happily went there on his second-hand electric bicycle to "safeguard justice on earth."

After the brothers arrived, Li Liang promised to treat everyone to hot pot after dinner and give everyone a pack of good cigarettes. Then, the atmosphere became tense...

Under the leadership of the big man Marin, the brothers found a few young yellow guys who were still setting up stalls. Then, like a knight, Marin spread his legs and launched a horseless charge...

As expected, the few yellow guys were all fighting against the five scum. They had beaten Li Liang in a group just because of the large number of people. Under the lead charge of the Marin Horse Knight, the little yellow-haired people were quickly beaten to a pulp.

But Marin was also carried away and just rushed forward without noticing the danger behind him. Then, a sinful brick appeared in the back of Marin's head...

Then, Knight Ma hit a brick on the back of his head and lost consciousness...

"Sure enough... Banzhu... Breaking martial arts... Shenya... can't stop it..." This was Marin's last thought...

........

After a long, long time, who knows how long...

Marin finally woke up, but something felt wrong. Because when he opened his eyes, he did not see the white walls and soft lights in the hospital, nor did he see the scene in his rental house... because he could clearly smell the green smell of grass...

"I'll beat you up! Li Liang, you bastard. My brother was injured for standing up for you, but you left him in the wild without even sending him to the hospital! Damn it, the labor and management are going to break off all relations with you!"

Marin cursed and tried to get up, but he suddenly realized that this relatively immature voice did not seem to be his, but it was indeed his own...

Marin, who has read many time-travel novels, is naturally familiar with this plot:

"Could it...couldn't it...have you traveled through time?"

Marin quickly stretched out his hand and scanned the parts of himself that could be seen with his eyes...

Then, he saw a green body, a strange costume, and a pair of snow-white hands that were obviously not his...

Suddenly, Marin's heart tightened - could she have traveled through time and become a woman? Not long ago, the movie "Prince Concubine's Promotion" was so popular that he was very afraid that he would travel through time and become a woman. What if she becomes a woman and is pressed down by a man in the future... Marin's hair stood on end and he didn't dare to think about it anymore...

With a nervous mood, he skillfully directed the "fifth girl" on the left to reach down to the lower parts...

After a while, Marin was greatly relieved - he was still a man, or so, he had time-traveled into a boy. Because the hair down there hasn't even grown yet..

With his mind relaxed and a strong sense of hunger coming over him, Marin fainted again...

..

After a long time, Marin woke up from hunger...

At the same time, a memory merged with Marin's consciousness - obviously, it was the memory of the original owner of this body...

By reading the memory, Marin learned that the original owner of this body was also named Marin. Of course, it is not the Chinese Marin, but the Western Marin. To be more precise, it was a young wandering knight named Marin Hoffmann in Germany at the end of the 15th century.

What is a wandering knight? First of all, this is a knight (mostly a trainee knight), who usually owns a war horse, a lance, and a set of knight armor. Some knights and war horses have horse armor, which is basic equipment. In addition, all knights have basically received strict training since childhood. They are good at charging immediately and are familiar with close combat. Their skills are generally much stronger than ordinary people.

In the European Middle Ages and Renaissance, knights were the lowest feudal lords in Europe. On it were barons, viscounts, earls, marquises, dukes, princes, kings, and emperors.

However, not every knight is lucky enough to get a fief. Because knights need to fight and fight for higher-level lords in order to have a chance to obtain fiefs.

Moreover, knights' fiefdoms are generally relatively small, only the size of one or several villages. For example, this little wandering knight named Marin, his family, the Hoffmann family, is a small knight family under the Mark County in the Ruhr River region. This family only owns a small village with little arable land, and the total arable land is only 100 eugrams (an ancient Roman unit of area, in the Middle Ages, one eugram was equivalent to 2 acres, equivalent to 12 acres). In other words, the Hoffman family only has 1,200 acres of land.

1,200 acres of land seems like a lot, right? In the Ming Dynasty at the same time, a large family would definitely have enough food and clothing to live in peace and prosperity. However, thanks to the unlucky agricultural technology of medieval Europeans, agricultural output is so low that it is shocking that the 1,200 acres of land of the Hoffman family are left fallow (Europeans practice a rotational fallow farming system, and the land is generally cultivated every two years) Or leave it idle for three years and one year to restore the soil fertility. Until modern times, this tradition still exists in European and American rural areas). Not counting the 600 acres of land, the remaining 600 acres of land can only produce 100,000 pounds of rye per year (a Cologne pound, one Cologne mark = 233.85 grams, one colon pound = 2 marks = 467.7 grams). On average, the rye yield per mu of land is only 166.7 pounds, equivalent to 156 city catties. This is still a good harvest year. If there is a famine, the yield will be even lower, and the yield per mu will drop to about 100 kilograms.

This kind of yield per acre is so touching. No wonder thousands of people starved to death in Germany during the Middle Ages...

What's even more impressive is that the wheat yield per acre in this era is even more impressive. In a large estate near Hoffman Estates, the wheat yield per acre is only less than 100 kilograms...

Damn it, as a top student in the history department, time traveler Ma Lin knows very well that since the Han Dynasty, the wheat yield per mu in China has been stable at around 200 kilograms. By the Ming Dynasty, rice in the south of the Yangtze River had a high yield per mu, even reaching about 500 kilograms.

Marin knew the crux of the problem from various time-travel novels - it was because Westerners didn't know how to use manure. For a long, long time, Westerners didn't know how to fertilize their fields. They just plowed the ground, sowed seeds, and poured some water. Then, he went to do other things. Even the serfs didn't know how to gather weeds.

As for feces, Europeans in the Middle Ages basically defecated everywhere, leaving cities everywhere with a stink wafting everywhere. This also caused flies to fly around and spread diseases everywhere. The Black Death that broke out several times in the Middle Ages was inseparable from the poor sanitary environment in Europe at that time.

In the East at the same time, farmers even pushed dung carts to cities to collect dung to use as fertilizer for farmland. There are also children who carry baskets to collect dung and eggs.

The Chinese people's excrement was put into the fields, which created the high yield of ancient Chinese agriculture and supported the prosperity of Chinese civilization. The feces of Europeans are all over the city, breeding a large number of flies, causing the raging of various diseases in Europe...

Well, Marin felt that he was thinking too much. The reason why his predecessor in this body became a wandering knight is a German tradition.

Since primogeniture was established in Europe, other sons of nobles have suffered. The eldest son takes everything, and the other sons can only watch as the eldest brother takes it all...

If it were a noble family, the situation would be better. For example, although the king's other sons do not have the right to inherit, the king's family has a great business, and the other sons can be given the title of duke, earl, etc., and they can have their own territory and continue to enjoy happiness. Dukes and earls and other great nobles have large enough territories, so they can share some meat with their other sons...

The little nobles were unlucky. Their family's territory was very small, only enough for the eldest son and his family to live. If you give some more to your brothers, everyone will have to drink the northwest wind. For example, the Hoffman family's 1,200 acres of thin land, with that little output, can barely feed the eldest son's family (not only eating, but also feeding war horses, buying and repairing weapons and armor, and feeding attendants, which is very expensive). It would be absolutely unaffordable to raise several younger siblings such as Marlin Hoffman.

Therefore, the knights, the smallest feudal lords in Germany, were forced to make a decision-when their sons except the eldest son came of age, they would give him a war horse, a set of armor and a lance. Go make a living for yourself. As for their daughters, some poor nobles even sent their daughters directly to monasteries to become nuns in order to save dowries.

Under normal circumstances, the children of these knight families who were kicked out chose to join the great nobles, such as serving as guards for the great nobles, fighting for the great feudal lords, and so on.

Of course, not every child of a knight who was kicked out of his home would have a good opportunity to find a noble employer who appreciated him. Many knights who could not find a job had to wander around looking for temporary employment opportunities. As a result, a new group emerged - wandering knights.

These wandering knights all grew up in knight families. They received strict martial arts training since childhood, and their skills on horseback were very good. It was not a problem for one of them to beat two or three ordinary strong men. Some strong men can fight off a group of bandits with one person and one horse.

In fact, these wandering knights were the earliest mercenaries. However, they are all individuals who seek opportunities to serve the great nobles and wealthy individuals as individuals and do not form a group.

It was not until a few years ago that Emperor Maximilian 1st of the Holy Roman Empire witnessed the powerful combat effectiveness of the Swiss mercenary army composed of Swiss mountain people during the war to compete for the Burgundy region, that he initiated recruitment in the German regions under his rule. The thoughts of a mercenary. It was during this period that the famous German mercenaries began to officially appear.

(Note: It was not until 1508 that Holy Roman Emperor Maximilian 1st was crowned by the Pope in Rome and officially became the Holy Roman Emperor. Before that, Maximilian 1st's title of emperor was actually self-proclaimed, and only the god Only the Roman Empire's own people recognized it. Without the Roman Pope's coronation, foreigners only recognized Maximilian 1st as the King of Germany. However, in 1508, Pope Julius II not only officially crowned Maximilian 1st, but also It is allowed that future emperors of the Holy Roman Empire do not need to come to Rome to be crowned, and can just find an archbishop in their own country to crown themselves.)

It is now 1494, an era when Emperor Maximilian 1st is recruiting troops to prepare for war. Marlin Hoffmann's father, Flins Hoffmann, after hearing that the emperor was recruiting mercenaries (Maximilian 1st had already recruited a group in 1487), decided to advance the plan. The second son, Marlin Hoffman, who was only 16 years old, was kicked out of the house and asked to defect to the emperor and become a mercenary of the emperor.

Originally, according to tradition, Marin would not be kicked out of the house until he turned 18. But the old Hoffman felt that the emperor was recruiting people and the opportunity was rare. If he achieved military exploits, he might even get a noble title and a fief. Therefore, he decided to let Marin take refuge early.

However, he overestimated his second son's experience in the world of martial arts - no, not long after leaving home, the original Marlin Hoffman only had 10 gold coins for travel expenses, and was bought by a captain of the guard who claimed to know the emperor. The scammers deceived them all...

Then, poor Marlin Hoffman had no money for a hotel or food. In desperation, he had no choice but to ride a horse and go hunting in the wild. First, the meat of the prey can satisfy hunger, and the fur of the prey can be exchanged for some money as travel expenses.

However, Marlin Hoffman is a knight, not a hunter. Therefore, little Marin couldn't catch any prey at all. Can you imagine a heavily armored knight, wearing heavy armor and holding a knight's lance, chasing and poking a galloping hare?

Wearing heavy armor itself will reduce the speed and sensitivity of the horse. How can it catch up with wild animals? Slow-running wild animals have long been hunted down.

Little Marin doesn't have a crossbow, and he doesn't know how to shoot an arrow. Therefore, he could only continue to chase animals on horseback while hungry...

Finally, after not eating for two days, the hungry little Marlin accidentally fell off his horse while chasing a deer and fainted in the grass. Then, he was time-traveled by Marin from China in later generations...
